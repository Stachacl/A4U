English: For the second hour I stand in a huge traffic jam on the road from Markhalivka to Kyiv. I took two journalists, the Guardian and the Telegraph, to Markhalivka, where they were writing about the Russian war against Ukraine. In Markhalivka, they filmed the consequences of a Russian plane rocket hitting a private house. Instead of a house - a pit. Several people are dismantling the wreckage nearby. I gave my earnings for three days of driving and shifting to a surviving man.

Ukrainian: Другу годину я стою у величезному корку дорогою від Мархалівки до Києва. В Мархалівку я возив двох журналістів Гардіан і Телеграф, вони роблять матеріал про російську війну проти України. В Мархалівці вони знімали наслідки потрапляння ракети російського літака в приватний будинок. Замість будинка -- яма. Поряд розбирають уламки декілька людей. Свій заробіток за три дні водіння і перекладання я віддав чоловіку, що вижив.

Stacha Clemens

  • Mother holding her dead child, Sensitive Content
  • oil on canvas
  • 60 x 92 cm
  • 2022
  • Estimated Value $750

War in 2022 looks like that.
I open my social media and I see my sister’s selfie with a beautiful smile on it is now replaced with a post about the war, she is in tears and there’s a fear in her eyes; photos or my friend’s cappuccino is now replaced with photos of destroyed buildings; videos of my favourite blogger’s overseas trips is now replaced with videos from overcrowded shelters for refugees, my favourite artist’s works are now replaced by heart breaking images of the distractions and pain that the Russian army has brought to average Ukrainian people. I can’t close my eyes, I can’t distance myself from it, I can’t unsee it. My heart is forever broken.

Vicki

  • The Nest
  • Digital Photo/Archival Pigment Print on Cotton Rag Paper 1/5
  • 60 x 90cm
  • 2018
  • Estimated Value $950

The Nest’ is part of a series of photographic works produced during The Wall Project Artist Residency, at the Manning Regional Gallery in Taree NSW in 2018. My project entitled ‘Traversing the Inner Landscape’ explored my interest in the emotional state of the human psyche through painting, drawing & photographing my physical reconnection with the landscape of the Manning Valley. The use of my own body & props became a theatrical performance of sorts, dramatising the inherent symbolism in the imagery & inviting the viewer to consider the notion of landscape as a “living source of wisdom, beauty & transformative power”.
